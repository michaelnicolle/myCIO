We build upward, foundations first. Here is what sits at each layer.

01
Top of the stack

Business outcomes.

Revenue. Efficiency. Uptime. The edge over the business across town. This is the only layer that actually matters to an owner, and it is where almost no technology conversation starts.

We work from the outcome down. We start with what you need the business to do differently, then work through the layers to the technology that gets you there. If a decision does not serve your business, we do not make it.

02
Where growth happens

AI and automation.

This is the layer everyone is chasing right now. Most businesses are stuck at the shallow end, a few people using ChatGPT for one-off tasks. Useful, but it is not moving the business. The value sits in AI woven through how the work actually gets done.

You cannot get there by buying a tool. It only works when the layers underneath are solid, which is exactly where most attempts fall over. We work out where you are, where you need to go, and the order to do it in. Using AI was never the point. Being quicker and sharper than the business across town is.

03
The layer everyone skips

Your data and knowledge.

This is the layer almost every business skips, and the reason their AI ambitions stall. The way things really get done lives in the heads of a handful of long-serving people. The business runs on it. It also stops the day one of them leaves.

AI is only ever as good as the knowledge it can reach. Without this layer, every initiative is working with whatever someone remembers to paste in. We help you capture what your people know, structure the data you already have, and turn it into a foundation your business can actually build on. This is the unglamorous work that makes everything above it possible.

Where it all starts

Networking, servers, cloud, Microsoft 365, the technology in every site, and the security wrapped around all of it. This is the plumbing. It is invisible when it works and impossible to ignore when it does not. Get it wrong and nothing above it holds.

This is where most businesses are quietly exposed, and where the years on the hamster wheel pile up technical debt. It is the work we know best. We build foundations to a seven-year standard. Stable, secure, and never dependent on any single vendor or person to keep them running, including us. Solid here, and every layer above it has a chance.

Where your business knowledge lives decides what AI can do.

It sits in one of four places. The difference between them determines whether your business can scale, survive key people leaving, and make AI actually work.

In headsWhere most sit

The way things really get done lives with a handful of long-serving people. The business runs on it. It also stops the day one of them leaves.

CapturedA start

Documented somewhere. Wikis, SharePoint, shared drives, that one spreadsheet nobody is allowed to touch. Better than heads, but scattered and out of date within a year.

StructuredThe wall

Organised, queryable, and maintained as the business changes. Process, policy, product, and customer knowledge the business can reference and trust. Most businesses hit a wall here, because structure needs an owner and nobody owns it.

ConnectedThe foundation for AI

Your structured knowledge joined to the systems and workflows your business runs on. This is what AI and automation actually build on. Without it, every AI initiative works with whatever someone remembers to paste in.

Most New Zealand businesses sit in the first two. The move up is slow, unglamorous, and the work that makes everything above it possible.
