Five signs the gap is already costing you.

01

AI is on the agenda, but nothing has moved.

Pilots have been tried. Nothing has stuck. Twelve months from now the conversation will be the same, and the businesses that did figure it out will be further ahead than they are today.

02

The business runs on what is in people's heads.

The real knowledge lives with a handful of senior people. When one of them leaves, something goes with them. It is also why every attempt at AI or automation runs into the same wall. There is nothing solid underneath for it to work with.

03

Nobody owns the technology direction.

Decisions get made by whoever is closest to the problem. No strategy. No roadmap. No one accountable for where it is all going. Everyone owns a piece of it, and nobody owns the outcome.

04

Technology spend keeps climbing, and the board wants answers.

Invoices go up every year. Nobody can connect the spend to a business outcome. Risk, roadmap, cyber insurance, AI. The honest answer is "I will find out," and that answer is getting used more often than it used to.

05

Vendor contracts get renewed because nobody has time to review them.

Nobody has looked at what you are paying, what you are getting, or whether the terms still make sense. Vendors know this. They are counting on you not asking.

If two or more of these sound familiar, your technology is likely costing you more than it should. That gap does not close on its own.

Questions people ask

A few things worth knowing.

Where do you fit with our existing IT provider?

We own the technology direction and run the transformation and delivery programmes ourselves, the work that moves the business forward. The day-to-day support and operations stay where they work best, with your existing provider, your own team, or a partner we bring in. We direct them and hold them to their commitments. The one thing that does not change is who owns the direction and who runs the programmes. We are not a helpdesk or a ticket queue.

How does pricing work?

We quote and work in day rates, not hours, so you are not second guessing time spent. Assessments start at a three-day engagement. Ongoing advisory runs on a monthly retainer at a preferential rate relative to day rate work. One-off assessments and projects are scoped and priced before any work begins. No surprises.

What size of business do you work with?

If you are big enough to feel real pain from technology that does not quite work, but not large enough to justify a full-time CIO and internal IT team, you are likely a good fit. We typically work with New Zealand businesses in manufacturing, food production, distribution, and construction, often turning over between $5M and $50M.
