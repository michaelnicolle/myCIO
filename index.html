Four signs the gap is already costing you.

None of these show up on an invoice. They show up in lost time, stalled projects, and decisions nobody wants to own.

01

Nobody owns the technology direction.

Decisions get made by whoever is closest to the problem. No strategy, no roadmap, no single person accountable for where it is heading. Everyone owns a piece. Nobody owns the outcome.

02

The risk picture is invisible until something breaks.

Security feels like box ticking, not managed risk. Then the board or your insurer asks a harder question about cyber and compliance, and the honest answer is "I will find out."

03

Your MSP manages itself.

They handle the tickets and respond when things break. But nobody holds them to account, checks what you are paying for, or makes sure the work points somewhere sensible.

04

Projects stall, drift, and restart.

They kick off with good intentions, then lose momentum. Six months later you are looking at the same problem, often with the same vendor, and a budget that has quietly grown.

If two or more of these sound familiar, the gap is costing you. It does not close itself.
