Questions people ask us

A few things worth knowing.

What does a first engagement look like?

Three steps, no pressure. First, a Discovery conversation: 30 to 60 minutes to understand how you are set up today, where it hurts, and what good would look like for you. No prep needed. Then a Review: we outline a simple picture of your situation and a few ways we could help. Finally, you decide whether you want targeted help, a fixed piece of work, or an ongoing engagement. No hard sell, no long proposal before you have seen any value.

Do you replace our existing IT team or provider?

It depends on how you engage. For Strategy and Governance and Professional Services, we sit above or alongside what you already have. Your existing team or provider keeps doing what they do, and we make sure it is heading somewhere sensible. For Your Technology Team, we become your technology function. That does not mean replacing people for the sake of it, but it does mean we own the outcome end-to-end: strategy, operations, delivery, and security. We are not a helpdesk. We are not a ticket queue. We are the partner accountable for all of it.

How does pricing work?

Strategy and Governance is a fixed monthly retainer. Professional Services engagements are scoped and priced per project, with clear scope agreed before any work begins. Your Technology Team is one monthly fee covering strategy, governance, security, vendor management, and delivery. No additional labour costs, no PO requests on top. For large projects or on-site work outside Auckland, we agree the approach up front. No surprises.

What size of business do you work with?

If you are big enough to feel real pain from technology that does not quite work, but not big enough for a full-time CIO and internal team, you are likely a good fit. We typically work with multi-site businesses in professional services, construction, logistics, manufacturing, distribution, and primary industries, usually spending $5,000 or more per month on technology across tools, vendors, and technology services. Some clients start with a single project to solve a specific problem. Others come straight to an ongoing engagement. Both work.

Do you work with boards and executives?

Yes, and it matters. Many of the businesses we work with have a board or CEO asking hard questions about technology risk, insurance requirements, or why projects keep stalling. Part of what we do is translate the technology picture into language that makes sense at that level: clear risk posture, honest progress reporting, and a defensible investment story. If you need someone who can sit in a board meeting and hold the technology conversation, that is a core part of what we offer.

What if we need on-site support outside Auckland?

We have a long-standing network of trusted local partners across New Zealand, including in manufacturing and primary industry environments where corporate systems meet operational technology and field assets. For anything requiring hands-on work outside Auckland, we bring in people we know well and have worked with before. You deal with us. We deal with them. The accountability stays with myCIO.
