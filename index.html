How technology actually works

AI is the current buzzword. Cloud was before it. The cycle is endless.

None of it works if the business underneath is not solid. Most businesses buy technology top down. It works bottom up. Here is the order it actually runs in.

01 Top of the stack

Business outcomes.

Revenue. Efficiency. Uptime. Competitive advantage. This is where every technology conversation should start, and where it rarely does.

Most providers sell you the layer they happen to deliver. We start with the outcome you are chasing, then work down to the technology that gets you there. If a decision does not serve the business, we do not make it.

02 Where growth happens

AI and automation.

Most NZ businesses use AI as a tool individuals visit, not a layer running through the business. The value is not in the first one. It is in the second.

We work out where you are, where you need to go, and the order to do it in. Then we make it happen. The goal is not to be using AI. The goal is to be faster and sharper than the business next to you.

03 The missing layer

Your data.

This is the layer most businesses are missing. Knowledge trapped in people's heads, not in systems. The way things get done lives with a handful of senior people. The business runs. It also stops the day any of them leaves.

We help you capture what your people know, structure the data you already have, and build the foundation AI actually builds on. Without it, every AI initiative is working with whatever someone pastes in.

04 Where it starts

The plumbing.

Networking, servers, cloud, Microsoft 365, the technology in every site, and security. The foundation everything else depends on. Get it wrong and nothing above it holds.

This is where we are strongest, and where most businesses are quietly exposed. We build it stable, secure, and not dependent on any single vendor or person to keep it running.

Questions people ask

A few things worth knowing.

Where do you fit with our existing IT provider?

We own the technology direction and run the transformation and delivery programmes ourselves, the work that moves the business forward. The day-to-day support and operations stay where they work best, with your existing provider, your own team, or a partner we bring in. We direct them and hold them to their commitments. The one thing that does not change is who owns the direction and who runs the programmes. We are not a helpdesk or a ticket queue.

How does pricing work?

We quote and work in day rates, not hours, so you are not second guessing time spent. Assessments start at a three-day engagement. Ongoing advisory runs on a monthly retainer at a preferential rate relative to day rate work. One-off assessments and projects are scoped and priced before any work begins. No surprises.

What size of business do you work with?

If you are big enough to feel real pain from technology that does not quite work, but not large enough to justify a full-time CIO and internal IT team, you are likely a good fit. We typically work with New Zealand businesses in manufacturing, food production, distribution, and construction, often turning over between $5M and $50M.
