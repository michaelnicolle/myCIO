Five signs your technology is working against you

Most businesses do not know they have a technology problem until it becomes an expensive one. These are the patterns we see most often.

1
Your IT provider is reactive, not strategic.

They fix what breaks. Nobody is looking ahead, managing risk, or telling you what is coming before it arrives. The relationship is comfortable — but it is not moving the business forward.

2
You cannot answer the board's technology questions with confidence.

When risk, spend, or roadmap comes up, the honest answer is "I will find out." That is happening more often than it used to, and it is starting to show.

3
Projects finish late, over budget, or not at all.

The original scope made sense. Somewhere between kickoff and delivery, it stopped being anyone's job to see it through. The business absorbs the cost and moves on.

4
Security feels like something you hope is being handled.

You have tools. You have a provider. But if someone asked you right now what your actual exposure is, you could not give a straight answer — and neither could they.

5
AI is on the agenda but going nowhere.

Everyone agrees it matters. Nobody is driving it. Twelve months from now the conversation will be exactly the same, and a competitor will have moved ahead.

If two or more of these sound familiar, your technology is costing you more than it should — and the gap does not close on its own.

Most NZ businesses are spending significantly more on technology than they need to.

Not because they are buying the wrong things — because nobody is reviewing contracts, renegotiating vendors, or making sure what was set up three years ago still makes sense for the business today.

That is not a technology problem. It is a leadership problem. And it is exactly what myCIO fixes.
