Questions people ask

A few things worth knowing.

What does a first engagement look like?

No hard sell and no long proposal before you have seen any value. Week one is a focused conversation with you and whoever needs to be in the room. We learn how you are set up, where it hurts, and what good looks like. Month one, the quick wins are in motion, ownership is clear, and your vendors know who they are dealing with. What clients say most often around month three or four is "I do not think about IT anymore." Not because nothing happens, but because when it does, it gets handled.

Do you replace our existing IT provider?

It comes down to one question: is the provider worth keeping? If they are, we leave them in place and run the strategy above them. We own the direction and hold them to account, and they keep delivering. If they are the problem, we replace them and become your technology function ourselves. We migrate you off cleanly and own the result end to end. We will give you an honest read on which one you need, even when the answer is keep them. We are not a helpdesk or a ticket queue either way.

How does pricing work?

We quote and work in day rates, not hours, so you are not second guessing time spent. Our standard rate is $1,600 per day plus GST. Assessments start at a three-day engagement. Ongoing advisory runs on a monthly retainer, priced at a preferential rate relative to day rate work. One-off assessments and projects are scoped and priced before any work begins. For large projects or on-site work outside Auckland, the approach is agreed upfront. No surprises.

What size of business do you work with?

If you are big enough to feel real pain from technology that does not quite work, but not large enough to justify a full-time CIO, CISO, and internal IT team, you are likely a good fit. We typically work with New Zealand businesses in manufacturing, food production, distribution, and construction, often turning over between $5M and $50M. Often the common thread is meaningful spend on technology without a senior person governing how it is used.

Do you work with boards and executives directly?

Yes, and it matters. Many businesses come to us because a board or CEO is asking harder questions about technology risk, cyber insurance, or why projects keep stalling. Part of the value is translating the technology picture into language that works at board level. Clear risk posture, honest progress, and a defensible investment story. If you need someone who can sit in a board meeting and hold the technology and security conversation, that is core to what we do.

Do you resell licensing or sell products?

No. We do not resell licensing and we have no products to sell. It would compromise the independence that makes our vendor accountability work. When you ask us what to buy, the answer is the right outcome for your business, not the one that pays us a margin. That is the whole point.

What if we need on-site support outside Auckland?

We have a long-standing network of trusted partners across New Zealand, including for multi-site rollouts and in manufacturing and primary industry where on-site presence matters. For hands-on delivery outside Auckland, or across many sites at once, we bring in people we know well and have worked with before. You deal with us. We deal with them. The accountability stays in one place.
