What we do

From the foundations up to where growth happens.

Most are sold the top of the stack while the foundation underneath quietly buckles. We cover the whole stack. Strategy, AI, data, modern work, infrastructure, networking, security. From the layer everyone sees down to the foundation everything depends on.

How technology actually works

AI is the current buzzword. Cloud was before it. The cycle is endless.

None of it works if the business underneath is not solid. Your technology is only as good as the business it sits on top of. And your business is only as good as what survives your best person walking out the door.

01
What you are actually trying to achieve

Business outcomes.

Revenue. Efficiency. Competitive advantage. Every technology conversation should start here and work downward. Not the other way around.

Most technology providers are built to sell you technology. We are built to ask a different question. What are you actually trying to achieve, and what is the shortest path to it?

That lens comes from experience on the other side of the table. Elton sits on the board of Tael Solutions, named Emerging Advisory Firm of the Year at The Gap Reunion 2025. Real directorship. Real accountability for business outcomes.

When we talk about owning the outcome, we mean it the way a director means it. Not the way a vendor does.

02
The layer everyone is buying

AI and automation.

Most businesses are somewhere at the start of a journey they have not fully mapped. It runs through four layers, and the value gap between the first and the last is enormous.

It starts with prompting. Individuals using ChatGPT or Copilot to get better outputs. Useful. Not transformation.

The next layer is context. Custom GPTs and Projects loaded with your brand, policies, and prior work. The team gets consistent outputs.

The layer after that is data. AI connected to what your business actually knows. Quality out starts matching quality in.

The layer above all of that is an operating layer. AI stops being a tool your people visit and becomes a layer running through your business. With governance, memory, and the standardisation to scale.

Most NZ businesses sit between the first two layers. The move up is where the real value sits, and it is where most attempts fail.

03
The layer most businesses are missing

Your data.

Your business knows things. The question is where that knowledge lives. It sits in one of four places.

It starts in heads. The way things get done lives with a handful of senior people. The business runs. It also stops running the day any of them leaves.

The next layer is captured. Documented somewhere. Wikis, SharePoint, shared drives, that one spreadsheet nobody is allowed to touch. Better than heads, but scattered.

The layer after that is structured. Organised, queryable, and maintained as the business changes. This is where most businesses hit a wall, because structure requires ownership.

The layer above all of that is connected. Your knowledge joined to the systems and workflows the business runs on. This is the foundation AI actually builds on.

04
Where it all starts

The plumbing.

Networking. Servers. Cloud. Microsoft 365. Security. This is the foundation everything else depends on. Get it right and everything above it works.

Our approach is simple. We do not custom-design from scratch every time. We lead clients through a proven reference architecture, then right-size it to the business. Not enterprise overkill. Not small-business under-spec. The foundation that fits.

That means Microsoft 365 done properly, configured and licensed for how your team actually works. Servers and infrastructure on the right platform for each workload. Secure networking built for multi-site, multi-cloud, and the people who work from anywhere. And security wrapped around all of it.

Right-sized foundations. Standards that work. Vendors held accountable. That is what rock solid looks like.

We see the whole stack. We get the foundations right. Then we build up to where the real growth happens.

How we engage

Three ways of working with us.

01

Fixed-fee engagement

A scoped piece of work with a clear outcome. Used in the early stages where the priority is getting an honest picture and fixing the foundations. Priced before any commitment.

02

Monthly retainer

Ongoing technology strategy and governance advisory, embedded in the business. Used once the foundations are in place and the focus shifts to standardising, transforming, and keeping the business protected. The core of how we work.

03

Day-rate consulting

Defined ad hoc tasks or specialist input. Used when a client needs something outside their retainer scope. Quoted by the day, not the hour.

Every engagement starts with a structured discovery. You get a clear picture of your situation and a recommended path forward, including cost, before any longer commitment is discussed. If it is not the right fit, we will say so.
